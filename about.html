About Me

I have been very interested in technology from a young age. Shortly before high school I became interested in computer programming and started writing simple Perl scripts. During high school I ran a neighborhood computer service business. Following this I was able to obtain an internship at Argonne National Lab before college after I taught myself c++. During graduate school I interned at Delphix in San Francisco which is where I currently work. I have sought out and been involved in a number of cool projects over the years, and some of them are detailed on this site.

Software Engineering

The areas that I find most interesting are parallel computing, distributed systems, large scale data processing, and low latency/real time systems.

Recently I've become interested in devops, and I have invested time into improving the build and test processes at Delphix in order to make other developers lives easier.

Mathematics

My favorite math topics are linear programming, applied statistics, graph theory, and machine learning.
